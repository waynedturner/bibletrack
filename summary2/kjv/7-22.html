The virtues of God's Word...continued (Psalm 119:89-176)

Psalm 119 is an alphabetic acrostic Psalm. It is divided into 22 sections of 8 verses each. In the Hebrew, each verse starts with a word beginning with the letter of the Hebrew alphabet for that section...in alphabetical order.

Virtually every verse mentions some aspect of God's direction in the Believer's life.

Notice the different words (KJV) used to describe God's direction in this Psalm:

  • word(s): From Hebrew "dabar" and is used 42 times; meaning "what God says."
  • law(s): From Hebrew "torah" and is used 25 times; meaning "a body of prophetic teaching."
  • precept(s): From Hebrew "piqquwd" and is used 21 times; meaning "a thing appointed."
  • judgment(s): From Hebrew "mishpat" and is used 22 times; meaning "a decision in a case of law."
  • statutes: From Hebrew "choq" and is used 22 times; meaning "something prescribed."
  • testimony or testimonies: From Hebrew "edah" and is used 23 times; meaning "laws as given with divine warrant."
  • commandment(s): From Hebrew "mitsvah" and is used 22 times; meaning "the particular conditions of the covenant."
  • ordinances: From Hebrew "mishpat" and is used only one time; meaning "the act of deciding a case."
  • way(s): From Hebrew "derek" and is used 18 times; meaning "a road or path."

Verse 89 begins this reading with a strong promise regarding the permanence of God's Word, "For ever, O LORD, thy word is settled in heaven." While the world treats truth as something that is relative, the truth of God's Word never changes because it is settled in Heaven. Who wants a moving target, anyway? It's wonderful to know that our God in Heaven has given us a living book for direction as is stated in verse 105, "Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path." That promise gives the Believer confidence in knowing exactly what God expects from us as it says in verse 165, "Great peace have they which love thy law: and nothing shall offend them." The Hebrew word for "offend" there means that God's Word keeps us from stumbling.

Psalm 119 is a great chapter that instills confidence in the Book we, as Believers, carry as our guide to Christian living - the Bible.
